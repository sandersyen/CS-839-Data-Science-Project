<person>Laozi</person>, also rendered as <person>Lao Tzu</person> and <person>Lao-Tze</person>, was an ancient Chinese philosopher and writer.[5] He is the reputed author of the <person>Tao Te Ching</person>,[6] the founder of philosophical Taoism, and a deity in religious Taoism and traditional Chinese religions.

A semi-legendary figure, <person>Laozi</person> was usually portrayed as a 6th-century BC contemporary of <person>Confucius</person>, but some modern historians consider him to have lived during the Warring States period of the 4th century BC.[7] A central figure in Chinese culture, <person>Laozi</person> is claimed by both the emperors of the Tang dynasty and modern people of the <person>Li</person> surname as a founder of their lineage. <person>Laozi</person>'s work has been embraced by both various anti-authoritarian movements[8] and Chinese Legalism.[9]
